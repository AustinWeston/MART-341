How to Make Stuffed Shells


Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp. minced fresh parsley
  • kosher salt
  • 8 oz. jumbo pasta shells
  • 30 oz. whole-milk ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Romano cheese
  • 12 leaves fresh basil, cut into chiffonade
  • 1 large egg
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 8 oz. Parmesan, grated
  • 2 jars good-quality marinara sauce
  • 8 oz. mozzarella cheese, grated
  • Crusty French bread, for serving

Directions

  1. STEP 1: Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F.
  2. STEP 2: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta shells and cook for half the cooking time on the package; make sure not to overcook. Drain and rinse in cool water. Set aside.
  3. STEP 3: In a bowl, mix together the ricotta, Romano, parsley, basil, egg, some salt and pepper and half of the Parmesan. Stir until combined.
  4. STEP 4: To assemble, coat the bottom of a baking dish with some sauce. Fill each half-cooked shell with the cheese mixture and place face-down on the sauce. Repeat with the shells until the cheese mixture is gone. Top the shells with the remaining sauce. Sprinkle on the mozzarella and extra Parmesan.
  5. STEP 5: Bake until hot and bubbly, 25 minutes. Serve with crusty French bread.
